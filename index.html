GentriMap

Visualising Gentrification

Info Map Typology Initial Status

There is no comprehensive approach for measuring gentrification tendencies in different neighbourhoods spread over a city, especially in the context of Germany. Thus, the actors who are involved in gentrification and concerned by the consequences lack reliable information about its intensity and spatial distribution. This work develops a framework which quantifies change in the relevant dimensions and sets up a gentrification typology for clearly communicating the results of the analysis. Applying this framework in a city sheds light on recent local developments as demonstrated here with Munich and the period between 2008 and 2012.

The socio-demographic dimension represents change in the social status of the local population. The residential-economic dimension reflects change in type and affordability of the local housing supply that is offered on the website of ImmobilienScout24. Emphasised by revisiting the original definition by Glass, the socio-demographic and the residential-economic dimension represent the driving forces behind gentrification. The proposed gentrification typology assigns each neighbourhood one of the following four types:

  1. Above-average gentrification area: Neighbourhoods that experience actual upgrading and above-average, relational upgrading in both dimensions.
  2. Below-average gentrification area: Neighbourhoods that experience actual upgrading and below-average, relational upgrading in both dimensions
  3. Actually upgrading, not gentrifying area: Neighbourhoods that experience actual upgrading in both dimensions, though relational upgrading at most in one dimension
  4. Not upgrading, not gentrifying area: Neighbourhoods that do not experience actual upgrading in both dimensions, independent from their relational development

The legend on the right side has a horizontal axis that indicates development in the socio-demographic dimension and a vertical axis that indicates development in the residential-economic dimension. Strong gentrification tendencies are indicated by approaching the top-right corner while the category in the bottom-left corner represents neighbourhoods that exhibit no signs of gentrification. For more details on the applied methodology and algorithms that result in the two indices for each neighbourhood, have a look at the thesis.
